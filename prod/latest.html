How To Save

Flash games handle their own save files, but Emulated games are a bit trickier to save, follow the tutorial below.

Text Directions

Emulated game saves are handled by what's called save states. A save state is an exact copy of what you're doing in that moment, so it's like you never even closed the game. To save your save state, click the save icon in your game like below.

This will download a file called "(code name of game).state" KEEP THIS FILE as this is your save. The next time you want to load your save, just click the load save button.

Then simply select the image downloaded previously, and you're right back where you left off!

Controls

Flash games have no universal control scheme, just depends on the game, Emulated games however are a bit trickier. To access the controls page, click the controller icon in the game.

This will bring up a menu, that looks like this:

This menu may look daunting at first, but take a damn chill pill and read it. On the far left in gray, you have the game's controls. On the left white box, you have controller inputs, this would be if you plugged in an Xbox or PS4 controller. On the right white box, you have the keyboard's inputs. To change controls, just click the set button on the right, then press the cooresponding key you want to map.


OR, deal with it, arrow keys are directions, z/x/a/s usually do things. Then on N64 controls are t/f/g/h instead of arrow keys, and w/e/r are used too.
